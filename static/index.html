Every partner has exactly one General tab (answered once, company-wide — things like company background, safety/cybersecurity posture) and one tab per Product they offer (one set of answers per product — sensors, functions, and product-specific questions like camera/radar subsystem detail). Use + Add Product on the Partner Detail page for each additional product. A Patents tab and a Notes tab sit alongside General/Product N for IP tracking and free-text meeting notes.

A Product tab only shows the sections relevant to it — checking the "Camera" sensor pill reveals the Camera Subsystem/Perception sections, checking "AEB" reveals the AEB Function Detail section, and so on. Unchecking a sensor/function hides its section again, unless you've already answered questions in it — those stay visible (with a confirmation prompt) so saved work is never lost silently.

2. Rating a question

Inside each section's question table, every question gets a Status, a Partner Response (what the vendor told you) and L&T Remarks (your own internal note) — kept as two separate fields on purpose, mirroring the source evaluation sheets' "Vendor Response" vs. "L&T Judgement" columns.

Questions within a section are split into High / Medium / Low priority sub-tabs — High is what to cover live in the first customer meeting, Low is fine to leave as a written follow-up. Use these to focus a meeting agenda instead of working through a section top-to-bottom.

3. Grading a section, and the master domains

Question status tracks completion. Grading tracks quality — a section can be fully Verified question-by- question yet still graded "Developing" in substance, or vice versa. Two independent grading levels, both with a free-text justification field:

  • Section grade — inside each section's card, just under its question table. General sections grade once per partner; Product sections grade once per product.
  • Domain grade — the Master Category Grading card in the left sidebar of Partner Detail. One holistic grade per partner per domain (Company Background, Perception Stack & Function Maturity, Regulation & Compliance, Safety & Cyber Security, Hardware Capability, Patents & Innovation) — a deliberate independent judgment call, not auto-averaged from the sections underneath it.
NANot applicable / not assessed.
DevelopingEarly stage, significant gaps.
GoodSolid, meets expectations.
OutstandingBest-in-class, a clear strength.

Domain grades are what shows up on the Comparison page's heatmap table and radar chart — grade a partner's domains here first, then go compare.

4. Hero Products, Patents, and comparing partners
  • Hero Products card (left sidebar, below Master Category Grading) — star-tag whichever of a partner's products are its differentiating ones. A partner can have more than one hero product.
  • Patents tab — track IP per partner (title, status from Not Filed through Granted/Expired, what sensor/function/area it relates to). Not part of the question/grading flow — pure metadata.
  • Comparison page — a heatmap table (rows = the 6 domains, columns = partners, color-coded by domain grade) plus a radar chart overlaying up to 4 partners at once on the same domains. Both read the domain grades you set in step 3 — nothing here is computed from question completion %.
  • Decision Log page — the running record of Shortlisted/Hold/Rejected calls and their rationale, set from the Decision card on Partner Detail.
